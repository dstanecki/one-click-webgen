10 Fun Facts About Capybaras

Fact 1

Capybaras are the largest rodents in the world, reaching up to 4 feet in length and weighing up to 145 pounds.

Fact 2

They are native to South America and live near bodies of water, such as rivers and lakes.

Fact 3

Capybaras are excellent swimmers and can hold their breath underwater for up to five minutes.

Fact 4

They are social animals and live in groups of 10 to 20 individuals.

Fact 5

Capybaras communicate using a variety of sounds, including barks, whistles, and purrs.

Fact 6

They have a diet that primarily consists of grasses and aquatic plants.

Fact 7

Capybaras have a lifespan of 8 to 10 years in the wild.

Fact 8

They have adapted to a semi-aquatic lifestyle and have webbed feet for swimming.

Fact 9

Capybaras are crepuscular animals, which means they are most active during dawn and dusk.

Fact 10

Despite their size, capybaras are quite agile and can run as fast as a horse.
