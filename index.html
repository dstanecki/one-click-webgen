10 Fun Facts About Ducks

Fact 1

Ducks are birds. They are also called 'waterfowls' because they are normally found in places with water, like ponds, streams and rivers.

Fact 2

Ducks have a wide range of geographical distribution, with species in every continent except Antarctica.

Fact 3

A male duck is called a 'drake', a female duck a 'hen', and a baby duck a 'duckling'.

Fact 4

Ducks have a variety of different species, including the Mallard, the Wood Duck, and the Northern Pintail duck.

Fact 5

Ducks have webbed feet which act like paddles for them to swim in the water.

Fact 6

Most ducks are monogamous for a breeding season but they do not mate for life.

Fact 7

Ducks have excellent vision. They have three eyelids and can see in color.

Fact 8

The feathers of ducks are waterproof. There is a special gland that produces oil near the tail that spreads and covers the outer coat of feathers.

Fact 9

Ducks are omnivorous. They feed on aquatic plants, small fish, insects, and grasses.

Fact 10

The 'quack' of a duck does echo, contrary to popular belief.
