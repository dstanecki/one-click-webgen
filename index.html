Fun Facts About Bunnies

Fact 1

Bunnies are crepuscular, which means they are most active during dawn and dusk. This is a natural instinct to avoid predators who hunt during the day and night.

Fact 2

Rabbits have an excellent sense of smell, hearing and vision. They have nearly 360° panoramic vision, allowing them to detect predators from all directions. They can see everything behind them and only have a small blind spot in front of their nose.

Fact 3

Rabbits are social animals that live in medium-sized groups known as colonies. The wild European rabbit lives in intricate burrow systems, called warrens, for protection against predators.

Fact 4

Rabbits' teeth never stop growing. They keep them worn down by gnawing on bark and other hard foods. In nature, rabbits would eat a variety of foods that would help grind down their teeth and keep their growth in check.

Fact 5

Rabbits perform an athletic leap, known as a 'binky', when they're happy — performing twists and kicks in mid-air!
