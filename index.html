10 Obscure Fun Facts About Ducks

Fact 1: Ducks Have Waterproof Feathers

Ducks have a special gland located near their tail that produces oil. They spread this oil onto their feathers to make them waterproof.

Fact 2: Ducks Have Excellent Vision

Ducks have a field of vision of nearly 340 degrees and can see in full color.

Fact 3: Ducks Can Live Up to 20 Years

With proper care, some species of ducks can live up to 20 years or more.

Fact 4: Ducks Have Been Domesticated for Thousands of Years

Ducks have been domesticated as pets and farm animals for more than 500 years, and all domestic ducks are descended from either the Mallard or the Muscovy duck.

Fact 5: Ducks Don't Feel Cold in Their Feet

Ducks have a counter-current heat exchange system in their legs, which means that their feet do not lose heat and do not feel cold even in icy water.

Fact 6: Ducks Can Fly at High Speeds

Some species of ducks can fly up to 60 miles per hour.

Fact 7: Ducks Have a Varied Diet

Ducks are omnivores and eat a variety of foods including plants, insects, fish, and even small mammals.

Fact 8: Ducks Have a Complex Social Structure

Ducks have a complex social structure, including a pecking order, and they form strong bonds with other ducks.

Fact 9: Ducks Migrate Long Distances

Many species of ducks migrate long distances every year to breed or find food.

Fact 10: Ducks Have a Unique Communication System

Ducks communicate using a variety of sounds and signals. For example, a duck's quack can convey different messages depending on the volume, pitch, and rhythm.
