Why Were Ducks Domesticated?

Ducks were first domesticated by humans hundreds of years ago for their eggs, meat, and feathers. They were easy to care for, fed on a variety of food sources, and bred quickly, making them an ideal choice for domestication.

10 Fun Facts About Ducks

1. Ducks are found on every continent except Antarctica.

2. The word 'duck' comes from the Old English 'duce' meaning 'diver'.

3. Ducks have excellent vision, they can see in full color and have a field of vision of nearly 340 degrees.

4. The longest recorded flight of a duck is 16 hours, covering over 800 miles.

5. Some species of ducks can live up to 20 years.

6. Ducks have been known to fly at altitudes of up to 21,000 feet.

7. The feet of a duck are cold resistant, thanks to a special circulatory system that prevents the blood from freezing.

8. Ducks have three eyelids.

9. The 'quack' of a duck does echo, contrary to popular belief.

10. The Mallard is the most common duck species in the world.
