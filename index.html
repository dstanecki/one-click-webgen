10 Fun Facts About Capybaras

Did You Know?

  • Capybaras are the largest rodents in the world.
  • They are native to South America.
  • Capybaras are semi-aquatic animals that love water and can hold their breath for up to five minutes.
  • They have a lifespan of 8 to 10 years in the wild.
  • Capybaras eat a variety of plants and fruits, as well as tree bark and aquatic plants.
  • They can weigh up to 140 pounds.
  • Capybaras have a gestation period of about 130 to 150 days, and usually have litters of four capybara pups.
  • Despite their size, capybaras are excellent swimmers and often escape predators by diving into water.
  • They have a calm and friendly nature, which makes them popular in petting zoos and sometimes as pets themselves.
  • Capybaras communicate through a combination of scent and sound marking.
