10 Fun Facts About Ducks

Fact 1

Ducks are birds in the family Anatidae. Ducks are closely related to swans and geese, which are in the same family.

Fact 2

Ducks have a wide range of habitats such as freshwater and sea water, and they can be found all over the world except for Antarctica.

Fact 3

Ducks have a variety of different breeds, sizes and colors. Domestic ducks are generally larger, and have a broader range of colors and patterns, than wild ducks.

Fact 4

Most ducks have a lifespan of 5-10 years. However, the oldest recorded duck lived to be 27 years old.

Fact 5

Ducks have excellent vision. They have three eyelids and can see in color.

Fact 6

Ducks are omnivorous, and they eat a wide variety of foods including plants, grasses, insects, and small fish.

Fact 7

Ducks are known for their quack, but not all ducks sound the same. Some ducks whistle, some grunt, and some are silent.

Fact 8

Ducks have been domesticated as pets and farm animals for more than 500 years, and all domestic ducks are descended from either the Mallard or the Muscovy duck.

Fact 9

Ducks have waterproof feathers due to an intricate feather structure and a waxy coating that is spread on each feather while preening. A duck's feathers are so waterproof that even when the duck dives underwater, its downy underlayer of feathers will stay completely dry.

Fact 10

Ducks have highly specialized bills that allow them to forage in mud and silt. The edges of the bill are fringed to strain plants, seeds, and small animals from water, mud, and pebbles.
