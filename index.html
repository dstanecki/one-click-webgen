10 Fun Facts About Ducks

Fact 1

Ducks are birds. They are also called "waterfowls" because they are normally found in places with water, like ponds, streams and rivers.

Fact 2

Ducks have a wide range of geographical distribution, with species in every continent except Antarctica.

Fact 3

A male duck is called a drake, a female duck a hen, and a baby duck a duckling.

Fact 4

Ducks have webbed feet which act like paddles for them to swim in the water.

Fact 5

Some species of ducks migrate or travel longs distances every year to breed. Usually they travel to warmer areas or where the food supply is plentiful.

Fact 6

The duck's mouth is called a "bill". Normally, it is broad and flat and has rows of fine notches along the edge called "lamellae". The lamellae helps the duck to grip its food so that it will not slip off.

Fact 7

Most ducks are aquatic birds. They spend much of their time swimming and may also feed on the water's surface, or dive beneath it to find food.

Fact 8

Ducks are omnivorous. They feed on aquatic plants, small fish, insects, and grasses.

Fact 9

Ducks provide us with eggs, meat and feathers. Their feathers, particularly the eider duck's, are used to fill pillows and quilts.

Fact 10

Ducks have been domesticated as pets and farm animals for more than 500 years, and all domestic ducks are descended from either the mallard or the Muscovy duck.
